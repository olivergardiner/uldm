Sales Transaction

In its most basic form, a Sales Transaction is an exchange of products (goods and/or services) and monetary funds in the context of an applicable legislatory framework that may impose charges or constraints on the transaction (e.g. sales tax). This view of a Sales Transaction is, however, abstracted from the specifics of how the transaction is enacted. Consequently, this domain also includes sub-domains to describe the elements of the transacting process specific to the following contexts:

  • PointOfSale - the purchasing of products in person in a physical store
  • CustomerOrder - the purchasing of products through an order placed for fulfilment (e.g. placed online, by telephone, or some other mechanism)

Note that a Sales Transaction typically involves the supply of products by the retailer to the customer for an agreed fee, but this situation may reversed in the context of a return which may be thought of as a form of compensating transaction that reverses the effects of some or all of the original transaction.
